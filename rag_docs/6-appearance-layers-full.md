# Character Appearance & Layers

The engine uses a deeply nested paper-doll sprite system (LPC format). Characters are assembled dynamically by stacking various layers.

## Standard Component Layer Contribution
The appearance object allows you to attach various graphical layers. Because they stack on each other, order matters (e.g., body first, clothing over it).
- **body**: The foundational base skin of the entity. Required.
- **head**: The shape of the head. Required.
- **hair**: The character's hairstyle.
- **torso**: Shirts, armour, vests. Covers the upper body. 
- **dress**: Full body robes or dresses.
- **legs**: Pants, skirts, armor plates covering the lower body.
- **feet**: Shoes or boots.
- **neck**: Bowties, neckties, scarfs around the neck.
- **facial**: Eyepatches, glasses, masks, etc.
- **beards**: Facial hair accessories like mustaches and beards.
- **hat**: Helmets, bandanas, pirate hats, wizard hats.
- **arms**: Sleeves or hand coverings.
- **gloves**: Hand coverings. (Aliases to the `arms` folder internally).
- **cape**: Capes and cloaks worn on the back.
- **shield**: A shield carried in the off-hand for defense.
- **weapon**: A weapon held in the primary hand (e.g. wands, swords).
